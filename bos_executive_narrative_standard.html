BOS Executive Narrative Standard™

"BOS describes organizations as systems of capabilities rather than collections of problems."

The 8 Immutable Editorial Principles

1. Observation before opinion

Facts always precede interpretation. Start with the evidence.

2. Capabilities—not people

Focus on organizational dynamics, never specific individuals.

3. Business language only

HR terminology is prohibited unless evidence-backed.

4. Neutral confidence

Avoid certainty. Use probabilistic, evidentiary language.

5. Value-dense prose

Every paragraph must answer a single executive question.

6. Systemic focus

Shift from blame/judgment to system design.

7. Precision, not exaggeration

Avoid hyperbole. Use calibrated, descriptive metrics.

8. Strategic context

Conclude with consideration, not empty recommendation.
